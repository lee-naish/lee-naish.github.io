ICLP'97 Submissions

The Call For Papers contains general information about the scope of the conference and the low-tech submission procedure. However, its probably easier for everyone, including you, to use a bit more technology.

Kinds of submissions

I am allowing three kinds of submissions:
  1. Hardcopy (multiple copies sent via snail-mail),
  2. Postscript file sent via e-mail, and
  3. Uuencoded DVI file sent via e-mail.
The advantage of electronic submissions is they can be transmitted much more quickly and cheaply. You could, for example, spend the weekend working on your submission and e-mail it some time on Sunday December 1st and still make the deadline. The disadvantage is that formats are not completely portable. For this reason, a backup hardcopy must be send in the mail (you have to send a letter anyway), though it does not have to arrive by the December 1st deadline.

Registering submissions

I am requesting that all people submitting papers register their submission via the Web. If you want to submit via e-mail you must register your submission via the Web. To register a submission, point your Web browser at this form, fill in the details of your paper (title etc), the type of submission, and your contact details and submit the form. Your browser will then display a covering letter which you can print, sign and include with your hardcopy submission.

Electronic submissions

The aim is to minimise the number of papers which can be printed by the authors but cannot be printed when they arrive in Melbourne or wherever the referees are (which could be anywhere in the world). Experts on non-portability of Postscript/DVI are quite rare it seems (if you are one please let me know!) so the following guidelines are based rumours rather than fact.

  • If you are using Latex without encapsulated Postscript then submit a uuencoded DVI file - although DVI is officially not as standard as Postscript it can actually be more portable.
  • If possible, try printing on A4 sized paper rather than USA letter size. Printing on A4 paper on an Apple LaserWriter II would be ideal.
  • Use reasonably large margins - I would prefer a paper which can print but is slightly over the page limit to one which cannot be printed. Note that the (strict) page limit on the final version of the paper will be shorter than the page limit on submissions so it wouldn't hurt to shorten your paper before its submitted!
  • Avoid obscure fonts.
  • Check your e-mail regularly after submitting. If we can't print your submission we might ask that you try sending a different format.
Lee Naish (ICLP'97 programme chair)
